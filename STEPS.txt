---

### **1. Define the Problem**

Decide what you want to predict.
**Example**: Predict whether a student will **Pass** or **Fail** based on features like study hours, attendance, and previous scores.

---

### **2. Prepare the Dataset**

* Collect your data in a structured form (like a table).
* Each row is a sample (observation).
* Each column is a feature (input) or the target (output).
* Ensure data is **clean** (no missing values or inconsistencies).

---

### **3. Split the Dataset**

* Divide your data into two parts:

  * **Training Set**: Used to train the model (usually 70â€“80%).
  * **Testing Set**: Used to evaluate the model's performance (usually 20â€“30%).

---

### **4. Train the Decision Tree**

* Use the **training set** to build the tree.
* The algorithm:

  * Chooses the **best features** (e.g., Study Hours).
  * Splits the data into branches based on decision rules.
  * Continues until all data is classified or a stopping condition is met.

---

### **5. Evaluate the Model**

* Test the trained model using the **testing set**.
* Compare predictions with actual outcomes.
* Use metrics like:

  * **Accuracy**
  * **Precision**
  * **Recall**
  * **Confusion Matrix**

---

### **6. Tune and Optimize**

* Adjust parameters like:

  * Tree **depth**
  * Minimum samples per leaf
  * Splitting criteria (like Gini or Entropy)
* This helps prevent **overfitting** or **underfitting**.

---

### **7. Visualize the Tree (Optional)**

* Plot the decision tree structure to see how decisions are made.
* Helps in understanding and explaining the model.

---

### **8. Deploy the Model (Optional)**

* Integrate the model into a software application.
* Use it to make predictions on new, unseen data.
